I'm an urban data strategist and geospatial analyst based in San Francisco, with a background in architecture and a Master of City Planning from Penn. I came to data through cities, which means I approach spatial analysis with an understanding of how urban systems actually work, not just how to model them.

My work sits at the intersection of geospatial analysis, data visualization, and urban policy. I build regression models, interactive maps, and data products that help public agencies, technology companies, and civic organizations make better decisions about how cities function.

I help cities and governments understand how they're investing in climate action and how to mobilize more resources toward net-zero goals, combining rigorous data analysis with compelling visual storytelling to turn complex information into clear decisions.

I've collaborated with Lyft's public policy team on bike share strategy across the Bay Area, collaborated with a project manager at Ocean Conservancy to produce a tool prototype, and delivered a data infrastructure strategy for the US Virgin Islands government to support climate resilience planning. My work spans mobility, food equity, climate resilience, and civic technology.

I'm particularly interested in work where spatial thinking is a strategic asset, where understanding how people move through, rely on, and are underserved by cities can shape better products, policies, and services.
